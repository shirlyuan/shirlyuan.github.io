Simply Me.

This is where you'll be updated on my latest piyo class, newest kitchen endeavors, and just little things that make me happy.

I baked a cake. An awesome cake.

I don't usually bake cakes. There isn't enough justification for pigging into something loaded with so much sugar and fat...not that I follow the recipe anyway. This was an exception. It was my roommate's birthday and I wanted to surprise everyone with the sweet waft of chocolate bliss as they walked in. Admittedly, I burnt the cake. The seconds it took to go upstairs and put my jacket away resulted in me spending the next half an hour scraping burnt pieces off the top. However, the effort was well spent. These gym hitting, salad munching divas each had hearty slices and we polished the rest off for breakfast the next morning. After all, it was peanut butter chocolate with organic granola crunch.

Kefir. A drink from the Caucasus.

So I have a problem. While I love dairy and cheese(smoked Dutch Gouda in particular), I'm lactose intolerant. In my quest to suffice my calcium intake, I stumbled upon this amazing probiotic drink: kefir!

My friend likes to refer to them as "sea monkeys". Yes, they require some nurturing and there were times when I woke up stumbling in the dark so I can strain them before heading to work. And a few Sunday nights when I've rushed home from weekends out to feed them fresh milk. But they pack a powerful punch: a single tablespoon contains over 5 billion beneficial bacteria! And while the tangy taste may take a bit to get accustomed to, they're fabulous for making pancakes, overnight oats and smoothies. So bottoms up!
